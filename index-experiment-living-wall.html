Scene I · Origin

Conquest enters as land.

The beginning is not grandeur but adhesion: a crossing, a marriage, a tract of ground, and the first obligation laid upon a name. What follows is not myth but tenure becoming memory over generations.

The line begins where land is taken, inhabited, and remembered.

Scene II · Witness

The archive survives because someone speaks.

What survives here is not chronology alone but custody. Voices endure because someone keeps them against loss: a woman with a letter, a steward with an account, a reader who refuses to let the page go dark.

A record survives only where somebody bears it through disappearance.
Enter The Family Tree Meet The Figures
Witness survives not as certainty, but as the light somebody keeps on a page.
Testimony is never neutral. It arrives lit, partial, and carried by a keeper.
This archive is not reference. It is something held long enough to outlast forgetting.
Scene III · Return

The hidden branch reads itself at the end.

The final movement is not revelation in the abstract but return in the blood. When Eleanor reads what Matilda preserved, the hidden branch does not merely learn something new; it comes back into the name it had been carrying all along.

The archive ends not by closing, but by recognizing itself.
