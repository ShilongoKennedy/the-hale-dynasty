The Wax Seal Collection

Nine wax impressions and seal matrices recovered from the MSS. Hale-Marsh Collection, spanning nine centuries — hover or tap each seal to read the archival identification

The seals below are reproduced from impressions found attached to documents in the MSS. Hale-Marsh Collection, or from matrices (the seal stamps themselves) found loose in the deed-box. The heraldic descriptions follow the standard conventions of the Bodleian's seal catalogue. The translations of the Latin inscriptions are the editors'. Hover or tap each seal for the full archival record.
— E.V., Oxford, 2024
Early Hale Seal
c. 1072 · Red wax · 38mm diameter
An early seal associated with the first Hale generation. Cross pattée over a crenellated tower.
Seal of Thomas Hale
c. 1370 · Dark red wax · Vesica form · 48×32mm
Standing figure, robes, lily sceptre. Vesica form — typically ecclesiastical or noble.
Seal of William Hale, Esquire
c. 1521 · Black wax · 44mm diameter
Armorial shield — chevron between three mullets. Used on the 1521 hall rebuilding deed.
Seal of Capt. Thomas Hale, broken
c. 1642 · Dark red wax · Cracked in three places
Found in two pieces in the deed-box. Sword and cross. Used on his last letter home before Edgehill.
Seal of Richard Hale, Esq.
c. 1741 · Red wax · Oval · 42×38mm
Monogram RH in cartouche. Used on estate correspondence during the Georgian period.
Seal of Augusta Hale
c. 1823 · Red wax · 40mm · Personal seal
Beehive (skep) device. Motto: industria opes — "by industry, wealth." Augusta's personal seal, not the estate seal.
Seal of Edmund Hale
c. 1865 · Black wax · Octagonal matrix · 38mm
Plain initial E. No heraldic device. Edmund's own choice — a departure from the family's armorial tradition.
Wafer seal — unimprinted
c. 1878–1882 · Red wax wafer · 30mm · No impression
Found sealing the outer sheet containing the two unsent letters (MSS. VII, items 37–39). No matrix was pressed into it. It is, and remains, blank.
Bodleian Accession Seal
1924 · Blue wax · 42mm · Institutional seal
Impressed on the outer wrapping of the collection on accession. Open book device. Not a family seal — the library's own mark.
