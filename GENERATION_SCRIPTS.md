**Script:**

> My sister wrote to ask whether I am quite well.
>
> I told her I am perfectly well. This is the answer one gives.
> The truth is that the entail has been confirmed, the estate
> is encumbered to a degree that cannot be spoken of in letters,
> and my husband's solicitor, a man named Pratt, came on Thursday
> and sat in the drawing room and looked at the furniture in a way
> I did not care for at all.
>
> I have been going through the household accounts myself.
> Pratt does not know this. I have found three errors in the
> last quarter that are not, I think, errors.
>
> I am perfectly well. I am writing everything down.

Stability: 0.80 · Similarity: 0.75 · Style: 0.08 (controlled, dry)

**Script:**

> The estate accounts for 1874 balance to within six shillings.
> I consider this satisfactory. My father would have considered
> it satisfactory. His father, who I am told kept the accounts
> in Latin until 1821, would also, I imagine, have considered
> it satisfactory.
>
> I have been thinking about the family record. We have kept
> everything. The letters, the inventories, the examinations,
> the indentures. We have kept everything and told it to
> almost no one.
>
> I am leaving the archive to my son. He does not know this yet.
> He is seven. By the time he is old enough to understand what
> he has been given, I will not be here to explain it.
>
> That is, I suppose, the condition of everything we inherit.

### VOL. IX — Edmund Marsh-Hale (WWII / Post-war, 1910–1978)
**File to create:** `audio/edmundmarsh.mp3`
**Era IX page:** `era-IX.html` — the audio player element is present,
add `src="audio/edmundmarsh.mp3"` to `<audio id="voiceAudio">`.

**Voice recommendation:** Male, British, matter-of-fact, 1940s register.
Should sound different from Victorian Edmund — less formal, more direct.
ElevenLabs suggested voice: *Bill* or *Harry* (British English).
Stability: 0.65 · Similarity: 0.78 · Style: 0.15 (slightly clipped, tired)

**Script:**

> The survey took three days. We went street by street.
> You write down what you see. Category A, Category B,
> Category C. You don't write down what it smells like.
> You don't write down the particular sound that a house
> makes when the roof has gone and the wind is moving
> through the rooms. You write: Category A. Structural
> integrity compromised. Estimated cost of repair.
>
> There was a house on Pallister Road. A woman was standing
> outside it. She didn't say anything. She just watched us
> write things down. I put her house as Category B, which
> meant it could be repaired. I don't know if it was.
>
> I kept the survey forms. My father kept everything.
> I suppose I'm the same. I kept the forms and the ration
> books and the letters, because if you don't keep them,
> nobody will, and then there will be nothing to prove
> that any of it happened.
>
> Which is the point, I think. That's the whole point.
